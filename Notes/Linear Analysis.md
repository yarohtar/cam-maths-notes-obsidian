### Theorem
Let $X$ be an $\infty$-dimensional normed space. Then there are some $(x_{n})\in X$ with $\lVert x_{n} \rVert=1$ for all $n$ and $\lVert x_{n}-x_{m} \rVert\geq 1$ for all $n,m$. In particular, $B_{X}$ is not complete.
