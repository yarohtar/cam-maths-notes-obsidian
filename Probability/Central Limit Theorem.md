Let $X_{n}$ be a [[Source]] of [[Independent]] identically distributed [[Random Variable]]s
with variance $\sigma^{2}$ and mean $\mu$. 
Define
$$
Z_{n} = \frac{ \sum_{i=1}^{n}X_{i}-n\mu }{ \sigma \sqrt{ n } }
$$
Then for large $n$:
$$
Z_{n}\sim N(0,1)
$$
#### Note
Letting 
$$
S_{n}=\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_{i}
$$
we find that the mean of $S_{n}$ is $n\mu$ and the variance is $n\sigma^{2}$.
Thus $Z_{n}$ has mean $0$ and variance $1$.
The theorem says that moreover $Z_{n}$ is normal.
