Orthogonal projector is a linear operator $P\in \mathcal{L}(\mathcal{H})$ s.t. $P^{2}=P$ and $P^{\dagger}=P$.

For any $\ket{u}$ s.t. $\braket{ u | u }=1$ we have that $\ket{u}\bra{u}$ is an orthogonal projector.

Let $\{ e_{i} \}_{i=1}^{n}$ be an orthonormal basis of $\mathcal{H}=\mathbb{C}^{n}$
Then
$$
\sum_{i=1}^{k} \ket{e_{i}} \bra{e_{i}}
$$
is the projection on the space spanned by $\ket{e_{1}}\dots \ket{e_{k}}$
