Let $K$ be a [[Field]].
The transcendence degree of $K$ 
is the cardinality of the largest [[Algebraically Independent]] subset of $K$.

### Lemma
Suppose $K$ and $L$ are [[Algebraically Closed Field]]s.
Then $K\cong L$ 
if and only if 
$$
\begin{gather}
\mathrm{tr deg}(K) = \mathrm{tr deg}(L) \\
\land\ \mathrm{char}(K) = \mathrm{char}(L) \\
\land\ \lvert K \rvert  = \lvert L \rvert 
\end{gather}
$$
### Lemma
Let $K$ be an [[Algebraically Closed Field]]. Then 
$$
\lvert K \rvert = \aleph_{0} + \mathrm{tr deg}(K)
$$
