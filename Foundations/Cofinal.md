Let $\lambda$ be a limit [[Ordinal]].
A set $C\subseteq \lambda$ is called cofinal if 
$$
(\forall \alpha<\lambda)\,(\exists \gamma \in C)\, \gamma>\alpha
$$
We define the set 
$$
\mathrm{cf}(\lambda)=\mathrm{min}\{ \lvert C \rvert : C\text{ is cofinal} \}
$$
### Lemma
$\mathrm{cf}(\lambda)$ is a [[Cardinal]] and
$$
\omega\leq \mathrm{cf}(\lambda)\leq \lambda
$$
Furthermore, 
$$
\mathrm{cf}(\mathrm{cf}(\lambda))=\mathrm{cf}(\lambda)
$$
### Lemma
$$
\mathrm{cf}(\aleph_{\lambda})=\mathrm{cf}(\lambda)
$$
