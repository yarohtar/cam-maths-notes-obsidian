Let $X$ be a [[Topological space]].
The [[Stone Space]] $S(\mathcal{P}(X))$ is the Stone-Čech compactification of $X$
and we write
$$
S(\mathcal{P}(X)) = \beta X
$$
Note that $\beta X$ is [[Compact]] [[Hausdorff]] by properties of [[Stone Space]].
Also $\beta X$ contains a cannonical copy of $X$
given by the inclusion map $x\to \hat{x}$.
### Theorem 
Let $K$ be a [[Compact]] [[Hausdorff]] [[Topological space]].
Let $f:X\to K$ be a [[Continuous]] map.
Then there is a unique continuous $\beta f:\beta X\to K$ satisfying
$$
f(x) = \beta f(\hat{x})
$$
This is a universal property TODO [[Category Theory]].
