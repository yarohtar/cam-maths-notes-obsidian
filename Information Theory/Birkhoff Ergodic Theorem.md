Suppose $X$ is an [[Ergodic]] [[Source]] on a finite alphabet $A$.
Then for any function $f:A^{\mathbb{Z}}\to \mathbb{R}$ such that $\mathbb{E}(f(X_{-\infty}^{\infty}))<\infty$ we have
$$
\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(T^{i}X_{-\infty}^{\infty}) \xrightarrow{\text{almost surely}} \mathbb{E}(f(X_{-\infty}^{\infty}))
$$
where $T:A^{\mathbb{Z}}\to A^{\mathbb{Z}}$ is the shift operator $(Tx)_{n}=x_{n+1}$.

[[Strong Law of Large Numbers]]
