Let $c:\mathbb{N}^{(2)}\to \mathbb{N}$ be a colouring (not necessarily finite here).
Then there is an infinite set $M\subseteq \mathbb{N}$ with one of the following:
1. either $c$ is constant on $M^{(2)}$;
2. or $c$ is injective on $M^{(2)}$;
3. or $c(ij)=c(kl)$ iff $i=k$ (for all $i<j$ and $k<l$ in $M$);
4. or $c(ij)=c(kl)$ iff $j=l$ (for all $i<j$ and $k<l$ in $M$).
