Let $X\subseteq \mathbb{N}$ be finite.
We say that $L\subseteq X^{n}$ is a combinatorial line 
if there is some nonemtpy $I=\{ i_{1},\dots,i_{r} \}$ (set of active coordinates)
and some fixed $a_{i}\in X$ (for each $i\not\in I$) such that:
$$
L=\{ x\in X^{n} : x_{i}=a_{i}\text{ for $i\not\in I$ and }x_{i_{1}}=x_{i_{2}}=\dots=x_{i_{r}} \}
$$

Also write $L^{-}$ and $L^{+}$ for the first and last points in $L$ 
(i.e. the ones with minimal and maximal $x_{i_{1}}$ respectively)
