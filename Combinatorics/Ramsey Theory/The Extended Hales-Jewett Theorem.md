**Theorem 10 (The Extended Hales-Jewett Theorem).** Let $m, k, d \in \mathbb{N}$. Then there exists $n \in \mathbb{N}$ such that whenever $[m]^n$ is $k$-coloured, there exists a monochromatic $d$-parameter set.

**Proof.** Regard $X^{dn}$ as $(X^d)^n$—a cube on alphabet $X^d$. Clearly any line in this (on alphabet $X^d$) is a $d$-parameter set on alphabet $X$, so we can take $n = d HJ(nd, k)$.
