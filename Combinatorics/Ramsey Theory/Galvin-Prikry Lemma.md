Given Y \subset N(ω), there exists a set M \in N(ω) such that either M accepts \varnothing into Y or M rejects all of its finite subsets.

Where M accepts A if (A, M)(ω) \subset Y, and rejects A if no L \in M(ω) accepts A.

## Proof

Inductively construct M1 \supset M2 \supset \dots with certain properties.
