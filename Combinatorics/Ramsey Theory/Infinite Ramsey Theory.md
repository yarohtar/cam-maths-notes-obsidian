Infinite Ramsey theory asks whether, in any finite coloring of the infinite subsets of $\mathbb{N}$, there is an infinite monochromatic subset.

The answer is no: there exists a 2-coloring of $[\mathbb{N}]^\omega$ with no infinite monochromatic set.

## Proof

Use the equivalence relation $L \sim M$ if $|L \Delta M| < \infty$.

Color based on parity of symmetric difference with representatives.

Note that in the above proof we used [[Axiom of Choice]].
