If $\mathbb{N}$ is finitely colored, there exist $x_1 < x_2 < \dots$ such that all finite sums $\sum_{i \in F} x_i$ for finite $F \subseteq \{x_1, x_2, \dots\}$ are the same color.

## Proof

Take an idempotent ultrafilter $u$.

Then $u$ contains a color class $A$.

Since $u$ is idempotent, $\forall x \in u \forall y \in u \, x + y \in A$.

Construct the sequence inductively.
