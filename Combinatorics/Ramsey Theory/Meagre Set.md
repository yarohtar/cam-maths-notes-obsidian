A set A \subset X is meagre or of first category if A = \bigcup_{n=1}^\infty A_n with each A_n [[Nowhere Dense]].

In complete [[Metric Space|metric spaces]], meagre sets are small by the [[The Baire Category Theorem|Baire Category Theorem]].

?-meagre sets in N(ω) have the property that for all A \in N(<ω) and M \in N(ω), there is L \in M(ω) with (A, L)(ω) \subset Y^c.
