Let $\mathbb{F}$ be a [[Field]].
Let $G=(V,E)$ be a graph on $n$ vertices.
The graph polynomial $f_{G}\in \mathbb{F}[x_{1},x_{2},\dots,x_{n}]$ is defined as 
$$
f_{G} = \prod_{ij\in E} (x_{i}-x_{j})
$$
### Lemma
Let $S_{1},S_{2},\dots,S_{n}\subseteq \mathbb{F}$.
Let $G$ be a [[Simple Graph|Graph]] on $V=\{ 1,2,\dots,n \}$
Then $G$ is $S$-[[List Colourable]] if and only if 
$$
f_{G}(s) \neq 0
$$
for some $s \in S_{1}\times S_{2}\times\dots \times S_{n}$.
### Lemma
Let $G$ be a [[Simple Graph|Graph]] on $n$ vertices.
Then $G$ is $k$-colourable 
if and only if 
The graph polynomial $f_{G}$ is not in the [[Ideal]] generated by 
$$
x_{i}^{k}-1
$$
for $i=1,2,\dots,n$
#### Proof
Let $S=\{ ^k\sqrt{ 1 } \}$.
#### $\impliedby$
Suppose $f_{G}$ is in the above [[Ideal]].
Then $f_{G}(x)=0$ for all $x\in S^{n}$.
Thus $G$ is not $k$-colourable.
#### $\implies$
Suppose $G$ is not $k$-colourable. 
Then $f_{G}(x)=0$ for all $x\in S^{n}$.
By [[Combinatorial Nullstellensatz]], we find that $f_{G}$ is in the above [[Ideal]].
